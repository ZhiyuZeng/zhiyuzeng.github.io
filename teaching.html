    Course Descriptions

    Text Mining (Spring & Summer 2024)

    This course explored methods for mining and analyzing large volumes of text data, a valuable resource in business operations, marketing, and public policy. Students learned key techniques such as text classification, summarization, sentiment analysis, and topic modeling. The course combined theoretical content with practical coding exercises in Python, using Jupyter Notebooks. A key component of the course was the group project, where students applied their skills to real-world problems, pitching and delivering actionable insights for businesses.

    Prescriptive Analytics (Fall 2023)

    This course focused on optimization models used in business decision-making, particularly in areas such as supply chain management, financial investment, production planning, and resource allocation. Students were introduced to linear, integer, and non-linear programming models and learned to solve large-scale problems using industrial-strength optimization tools like Python and Excel Solver. The course was structured as a flipped classroom, where students engaged with lecture videos before applying the material in weekly lab sessions under my guidance.
